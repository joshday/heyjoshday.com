MM Algorithms

Definition

Majorization-Minimization (MM) is an important concept in optimization. It is more of a principle than a specific algorithm, and many algorithms can be interpreted under the MM framework (coordinate descent, proximal gradient method, EM Algorithm, etc.). The main idea is the concept of a majorizing function.

A Function h(θ)h(\theta)h(θ) is said to majorize f(θ)f(\theta)f(θ) at θ(t)\theta^{(t)}θ(t) if

h(θ∣θ(t))≥f(θ∣θ(t))(dominance condition),h(θ(t)∣θ(t))=f(θ(t)∣θ(t))(tangent condition). \begin{aligned} h(\theta | \theta^{(t)}) &\ge f(\theta | \theta^{(t)}) \quad\text{(dominance condition)}, \\ h(\theta^{(t)} | \theta^{(t)}) &= f(\theta^{(t)} | \theta^{(t)}) \quad\text{(tangent condition)}. \end{aligned} h(θ∣θ(t))h(θ(t)∣θ(t))​≥f(θ∣θ(t))(dominance condition),=f(θ(t)∣θ(t))(tangent condition).​

Simply put, the surface of h(θ)h(\theta)h(θ) lies above f(θ)f(\theta)f(θ), apart from at θ(t)\theta^{(t)}θ(t) where they are equal. An MM update consists of minimizing the majorization:

θ(t+1)=argminθ h(θ∣θ(t)).\theta^{(t+1)} = \text{argmin}_\theta \;h(\theta|\theta^{(t)}).θ(t+1)=argminθ​h(θ∣θ(t)).

Things to note:

  • MM updates are guaranteed to decrease the value of the objective function (descent property).

  • Majorizations are often used to split parameters, allowing updates to be done element-wise.

Majorization

One technique for majorizing a convex function is a quadratic upper bound. If we can find a matrix MMM such that M−d2f(θ)M - d^2f(\theta)M−d2f(θ) is nonnegative definite for all θ\thetaθ, then

Using the RHS as our majorization, updates take the form

θ(t+1)=θ(t)−M−1∇f(θ(t)).\theta^{(t+1)} = \theta^{(t)} - M^{-1}\nabla f(\theta^{(t)}).θ(t+1)=θ(t)−M−1∇f(θ(t)).

This method produces an update rule that looks like Newton's method, but uses an approximation of the Hessian which guarantees descent.
